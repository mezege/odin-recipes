Chicken Burger Recipe

Top tips for making chicken burgers.

- How long do I cook them? The trick to cooking a good chicken burger is watching the cook time These burgers can go from juicy to bone dry quickly once overcooked. They should take about 4 to 5 minutes per side, but to ensure doneness, use an instant-read meat thermometer: check that the center of the burger reaches 165ºF, which is the USDA's recommended temperature for poultry.

- do I keep my chicken burgers from falling apart? Your patties should come together and hold together nicely while cooking, but we do suggest being careful with them while cooking. Don’t mess with them too much while they’re in the pan, and take care when flipping!

- How should I top them? We load these burgers up with crunchy, bright coleslaw before topping with creamy avocado slices, spicy fresh red onion, and cheddar and jalapeño before sandwiching all of it between buttery brioche buns for the ultimate bite.

YIELDS: 4 serving(s)

PREP TIME: 25 mins

TOTAL TIME: 45 mins

CAL/SERV: 772

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lb. ground chicken

    3/4 tsp. smoked paprika

    1 clove garlic, minced

    scallions, minced

    Kosher salt

    Freshly ground black pepper

    2 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil

    4 slices cheddar

    4 leaves butterhead lettuce

    2 c. coleslaw

    1 avocado, thinly sliced

    1/4 small red onion, thinly sliced

    1 jalapeño, thinly sliced

    4 brioche burger buns, split and lightly toasted

Directions

  • Step 1
    • In a large bowl, combine chicken, paprika, garlic, and green onions, and season with salt and pepper. Divide mixture into 4 patties.
  • Step 2
    • In a large skillet over medium heat, heat oil. Add burger patties and cook, flipping once, until golden and a thermometer inserted into the center registers 165°, 8 to 10 minutes. Top with cheddar, cover, and cook until just melted, 2 minutes. Remove from heat and transfer patties to a plate.
  • Step 3
    • Stack lettuce, coleslaw, chicken burgers, avocado, red onion, jalapeño, and more coleslaw on top of bottom buns. Close sandwiches with top buns.
